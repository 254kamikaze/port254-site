Situation

A regional water utility operating multiple treatment plants and a network of remote pumping stations was notified of its status as a responsible entity under the SOCI Act. The board commissioned a CIRMP, and the IT team was tasked with extending the existing ISO 27001-aligned security program to cover OT. Before that work could proceed, the organisation needed a clear picture of its actual OT security posture.

The OT environment had grown organically over 15 years — no formal security architecture, no documented zone and conduit model, and no prior OT-specific security assessment had ever been conducted.

Key Findings

  • Critical No network segmentation. The OT network — treatment plant control systems, remote pump station RTUs, and the corporate network — operated on a single flat network. No firewall or conduit existed between the corporate zone and OT control systems.
  • Critical Process historian directly accessible from corporate network. The historian had no DMZ or data diode separation from the business network. A compromised corporate endpoint had a direct read/write path to the process historian and onward to the control network.
  • Critical Persistent vendor remote access with no MFA. The primary SCADA vendor maintained a persistent VPN connection using static credentials. Access was never time-limited, audited, or reviewed. No multi-factor authentication was enforced.
  • Critical No OT monitoring capability. No network visibility or anomaly detection existed on the OT network. Security events — including unauthorised connections, protocol anomalies, and device changes — were entirely invisible to the security team.
  • High Shared HMI credentials across all shift operators. A single shared account ("operator") was used by all control room staff. No individual accountability, no session logging, and no mechanism to detect or investigate insider incidents.
  • High Engineering workstations running end-of-life OS. HMI workstations at the primary treatment plant ran Windows 7 — unsupported since January 2020, unpatched for over three years. Vendor constraints prevented OS upgrades without a full system replacement.
  • Medium Remote pump stations had no lateral movement controls. A compromised RTU at any remote site had an unobstructed network path to the treatment plant control zone. No conduit controls existed between remote sites and the primary control network.

Deliverables

Zone and conduit model — 6 zones documented across treatment, remote sites, IT/OT DMZ, and vendor access
SL-T assignment per zone based on IEC 62443-3-2 risk assessment
SL-A gap assessment across all seven foundational requirements per zone
Risk assessment with threat and consequence modelling for water treatment disruption scenarios
Risk-prioritised remediation roadmap — 18 findings across 4 critical, 7 high, 5 medium, 2 low
CIRMP technical annexure mapping IEC 62443 controls to SOCI Act cyber hazard vector obligations
Board-ready executive summary with risk posture overview and investment priorities

Outcome

The assessment gave the utility its first accurate picture of OT security posture — and a defensible technical foundation for its SOCI Act CIRMP. The zone and conduit model and SL gap analysis were incorporated directly into the CIRMP cyber vector as the technical evidence base for board attestation.

The top three remediation priorities — historian segmentation, vendor remote access controls, and OT network segmentation — were scoped and funded within six weeks of report delivery. The network segmentation project, estimated at 90 days, was approved as a capital project in the following budget cycle.
