About This Research

port254 is my personal research initiative documenting real-world attack patterns against industrial control systems. Using live honeypots deployed as full ICS environments, I collect and analyze actual threat actor behavior to build a freely-available detection rule catalog.

This isn't a commercial product—it's a research project aimed at improving cybersecurity visibility in operational technology domains. All findings, detection rules, and telemetry are shared openly with the security community.

🍯

Live Honeypot Telemetry

The foundation of this research is a network of full-stack ICS honeypots deployed in two geographic locations. Each deployment mimics a complete industrial environment with PLCs, HMIs, historians, and supporting infrastructure to attract and study real attack patterns.

Research Objective: Compare attack patterns between geographic regions. Do adversaries target Africa differently than Oceania? Which protocols are prioritized? How do attack timings differ across time zones? The dual-deployment approach allows direct comparison of threat landscapes.
